"예술가에게 필요한것은 단 하나의 열열한 시청자 뿐이다."

This study navigates the intricate terrain of gameness and playability from historical and philosophical perspectives. I bring Martin Heidegger’s philosophy of art and Jesper Juul’s game model into conversation to effectively capture the unique interactive essence of games. By synthesizing narratological and ludological viewpoints, I formulate a comprehensive understanding of games as a form of interactive art. For this purpose, I introduce new frameworks, including interactive act structure, game structure, and the structure of video game construction; these methodologies aim to guide designers in creating artistically significant games. The concept of interactivity is interwoven into these structures, providing a tangible means for measuring user interaction. This study underlines the critical role of literature and liberal arts in game design and the potential of games to function as a robust medium for artistic expression.
